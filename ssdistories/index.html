Help us find great stories for the Explore VR SSDI Video Project!

We are looking for individuals with a disability who are currently employed and who wish to share their success stories with others. In particular, we seek people who:

  • have received services from a state voc rehab agency,
  • are former Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) recipients, and
  • now earn above the Substantial Gainful Activity (SGA) amount.

We want to interview and videotape folks about their return to work and their transition off of SSDI cash benefits. Stories will be discussed with and filmed by staff from the Explore VR project at the Institute for Community Inclusion. We wish to showcase strategies that people with significant disabilities have used, perhaps with help from a voc rehab counselor or a community rehab provider, to return to work and get off SSDI cash benefits. We’re hoping that these videos will inspire other individuals receiving SSDI to re-enter the workforce at or above SGA.

If we choose to use an individual's story for our project, he or she will receive a $500 stipend as a sign of our appreciation.

We wish to include a wide range of individuals in this project, including those who...

  • Are under age 65
  • Have worked with a state vocational rehabilitation agency
  • Have received SSDI benefits because of severely disabling condition [The disability may include a mental health condition, a significant physical injury or health condition, dual diagnosis or co-occurring disorders, intellectual disabilities, deafness/ hearing impairment, nervous system disorders, and/or mobility challenges]
  • Have received SSDI in the past, but no longer receive SSDI cash benefits
  • Currently earn income above SGA ($1040/month, 2013 & $1070/month, 2014)
  • Have never participated in an Accelerated Benefits Demonstration Project or Study

Do you know someone who...

  • Has a compelling story that could energize/educate others about the positive benefits of returning to work?
  • Would be comfortable sharing personal experiences regarding this topic?
  • Is willing to be filmed in a discussion/interview format by ICI/UMB staff?
  • Can explain what helped encourage them to pursue a career?
  • Wishes to provide guidance and dispel fears regarding change?

If you know someone whose story we should hear, let us know.
