Results-Oriented Transition Assessment

Elena Varney
Jan Hollenbeck
Jill Curry

Learn about the Transition Assessment process from the experiences of Transition Coordinators working in a variety of school settings. Practical, in-depth information will be presented that will empower districts to individualize their transition assessment process. Participants will learn a process for identifying guiding questions, selecting effective methods and tools for answering those questions, and reporting action-oriented results and recommendations for team consideration. The importance of using a collaborative approach throughout the transition assessment process will be highlighted. The differences between transition assessments that are ongoing and those that require consent will also be discussed. Resources to guide the transition assessment process, including tool selection will be provided.

Developing an Interagency Transition Team: Outreach, Resource Mapping, and Collaboration

Dan Shaughnessy
Kelli McSweeney

The mission of the Shrewsbury Interagency Transition Team is to maintain a comprehensive support network for students with disabilities by fostering communication and collaboration between district staff, local business partners, and community agencies. The goal of the Shrewsbury Interagency Transition Team is to improve outcomes for students as they transition from public school to adult living, learning, and working environments. This session will focus on the process of building and maintaining an interagency team from the ground up. Topics will include recruiting, timelines, self-assessment, goal setting, and communication.

Transitioning Back, Transition Forward: Making Transition Services Relevant and Effective for Youth in the Child Welfare and Juvenile Justice System

Leigh Gallivan
R. Jeriko Perkins

Large numbers of youth involved with the juvenile justice or child welfare systems also live with disabilities.  For these students, the term transition can take on multiple and complex meanings, often with negative connotations.  But strong, consistent transition planning and implementation can be the pivotal factor in a student’s success.  This session will provide an overview of the challenges and potential solutions to developing and implementing comprehensive transition plans for youth with complex needs and living situations, as well as strategies for using the IEP to  leverage students’ many stakeholders to best equip them for the crucial transition to adulthood and independence.

Learning from Common District Mistakes

Arlyn Roffman

It has been more than a decade since the federal government strengthened IDEA’s focus on transition for students with disabilities on IEPs. Despite its clear mandate that districts conduct age-appropriate assessment to drive the development of individualized goals in the areas of employment, post-secondary learning, and community living, many school districts are still struggling to fulfil their obligation to follow required procedures and provide appropriate programming under the law. This session will focus on common district mistakes in transition planning and service delivery and on how they can be avoided.

Effective Practices in Schools around the Commonwealth for Career Readiness for ALL Students

Shailah Stewart

The Massachusetts Department of Elementary and Secondary Education has adopted a new definition for college and career readiness for all; now, being college and career ready means that a student has the knowledge, skills and experiences necessary for success in postsecondary education and economically viable career pathways in a 21st century economy. This session will take a look at 1) effective practices at a number of districts around Massachusetts designed to address that new definition by scaling up career readiness experiences for all students, and 2) several ESE-sponsored state-wide initiatives designed to help districts offer career experiences, so that all students leave high school equipped with the skills they need for “Success After High School.”

Preparing All Students for Success After High School: Understanding Secondary Transition in Massachusetts

Terri McLaughlin
Martha Daigle
Amanda Green

This two-part presentation will offer an overview of the secondary transition process for students with IEPs ages 14-22, including laws and regulations, the importance of student vision, the use of the Transition Planning Form (TPF) and IEP, and whole school/community approaches. Special emphasis will be placed on the essential role of the family in partnering with professionals to promote positive adult outcomes for their children.

Massachusetts College and Career Readiness Efforts

Lisa Harney

The Office of College and Career Readiness (CCR) contributes to the Department of Elementary and Secondary Education's (ESE) statewide effort to help prepare ALL students for "Success After High School." This session will highlight key priority projects that come out of the CCR office including the MassGrad program, Early Warning Indicator System (EWIS), MassCore, Connecting Activities, and Career Development Education. In addition, an asset mapping tool and a rubric for evaluating a school’s CCR programming will be presented.

College Options for Students with Intellectual Disabilities

Glenn Gabbard
Debra Hart

This session will focus on national and state options for programs supporting post-secondary educational opportunities for Individuals with intellectual disabilities. “Think College,” a national initiative supporting a network of colleges and universities will be discussed along with Massachusetts’ Inclusive Concurrent Enrollment (ICE) initiative. The Inclusive Concurrent Enrollment initiative offers academic and social learning opportunities in 10 public two- and four-year colleges across the state.

Technology & Apps for Transition

Lori Cooney
Nancy Hurley

In this workshop, participants will discover how the iPad is a necessary transition tool for students in inclusive higher education. Lori will share recommended apps that enhance executive functioning skills and incorporate universal design for learning strategies. You will leave this workshop with a variety of recommended apps being implemented in iPad initiatives in inclusive postsecondary education programs around the country. Additionally, participants will also recognize the importance of utilizing social media apps for student productivity, networking and success.

Going to Work: Social Security and Transition Aged Youth

Brian Forsythe
Christine Roa

This presentation will educate youth, young adults, parents, and providers around how work can impact Social Security disability benefits (SSI/SSDI) and healthcare (MassHealth /Medicare). Additionally, the audience will learn what may happen to a person’s benefits when they legally transition into adulthood at age 18.

WIOA: What It Means for Massachusetts and Transition

David Hoff
Jennifer James Price
Alice Sweeney
Kasper Goshagrian

The Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA), signed into law in late July, reauthorizes the Workforce Investment Act, including the Rehabilitation Act through 2020. This major piece of federal legislation is intended in part, to significantly enhance the ability of young people with disabilities to successfully transition from school to employment and long-term career success. This session will take a look at WIOA, the opportunities it presents, and its potential impact on schools, the public vocational rehabilitation system (MRC & MCB), the Department of Workforce Development, Department of Career Services, other public agencies and services, and most importantly on the lives of young people with disabilities. Topics discussed include the new requirements for “pre-employment transition services” and the increased focused on partnerships between workforce development, education, and vocational rehabilitation. The timeline for implementation of WIOA will be reviewed and opportunities to offer input and get involved.

Customizing employment options for transition aged students

Ross Hooley

The high school years are the perfect time for students to explore different jobs, gain work experience, or acquire an after-school job in their community. This presentation will share effective strategies used to develop individualized employment options. You will also see examples of job exploration and career planning, and learn strategies to help students acquire paid employment based on their work preferences and strengths.
Using Technology to Enhance Self Awareness for Transition Planning

Susan Gately

Many early adolescents show little interest in transition planning, as the transition to adult life seems far away. Answering the questions, “Who am I and what do I want to do with my life?” are abstract. Using materials adapted from Personal Preference Indicators* as well as other programs that focus on self concept helps shape students thinking about their feelings, social interests, social skills, daily living skills and making choices. During the progress, students work with peers, teachers, and family to develop the self awareness necessary for them to become self-advocating individuals. The process discussed here integrates technology which enhances student engagement and exposes students to technology skills. This session will demonstrate the template of the students’ portfolios using hyperlinks, pictures, interviews, and short videos that help them tell their story and demonstrate their skills. *(Center for Learning and Leadership, University Center for Excellence in Developmental Disabilities Education, Research and Service University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Centers)
